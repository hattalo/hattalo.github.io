Illative


The illative is one of the six paikallissijat or location cases. This case is usually used in the same instances when into or to would be used in English.

  • Singular
        •  
    • A word ending in a single -a, -o, -u, -y, -ä, or -ö
      • Use the basic stem and -Vn.
         
    • A one-syllable word ending in a double vowel
      • Use the basic stem or genitive stem and -hVn.
         
    • A multisyllable word ending in a double vowel (except for -ii)
      • Use the genitive stem and -seen.

        •  
    • A multisyllable word ending in a -ii
      • Use the genitive stem and -hVn.
         
    • A word ending in a dipthong
      • Use the basic stem or genitive stem and -hVn.
         
    • A word ending in a single -e
      • Use the genitive stem and -seen.
         
    • A word ending in a single -i
      • Use the partitive stem and -Vn.
         
    • A word ending in any consonant other than -n
      • Use the genitive stem and -seen.
         
    • A word ending in an -n
      • Use the genitive stem and -Vn.

        •  
       
  • Plural
      •  
    • A word ending in a single -a, -o, -u, -y, -ä, or -ö
      • Use the genitive stem and -hin.
         
    • A one-syllable word ending in a double vowel
      • Use the genitive stem and -hin.
         
    • A multisyllable word ending in a double vowel (except for -ii)
      • Use the genitive stem and -siin.
         
    • A multisyllable word ending in a -ii
      • Use the genitive stem and -hin.
         
    • A word ending in a dipthong
      • Use the genitive stem and -hin.
         
    • A word ending in a single -e or -i
      • Use the genitive stem and -hin, -Vn or -siin.
         
    • A word ending in any consonant other than -n
      • Use the genitive stem and -siin.
         
    • A word ending in an -n
      • Use the genitive stem and -hin.
